History & layout

The highest trullo, with a warm family atmosphere

Trullo Viola, with its six meters height, is the highest trullo of the structure. Originally it also had a wooden second floor supported by two robust olive beams, and was destined for storing the products of the earth.

It underwent a first restoration at the beginning of the 20th century, which added an external oven, a well for collecting water and a cellar for preserving cheese, oil and wine.

In 2010 it was restored again to its current form: a 50 m² house ideal for 2–3 adults or for a couple with 1–2 children.

It features a double bedroom, a living and dining area with two single beds separated by heavy curtains, a fully equipped kitchen, a bathroom and a private veranda equipped for outdoor living.
